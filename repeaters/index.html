Repeaters

W2CXV and K2QQY repeater systems serving Oswego County, NY

Callsign / Frequency Details
W2CXV
147.150 MHz		 Receive: 147.150 MHz   Transmit: 147.750 MHz   PL Tone: 103.5
Located at the Scriba Site, approximately ½ mile East of the intersection of County Routes 29 and 51A.
Coordinates: 43°27.336′N × 76°22.482′W. Antenna: 4-bay DB-234, ~685 ft ASL. ~20 W.
Trustee: Fred Koch KA2HPG. Open for all normal amateur activity.
Part of the communications backup plan for the power company.
W2CXV
146.850 MHz		 Receive: 146.850 MHz   Transmit: 146.250 MHz   PL Tone: 103.5 (not currently active)
Located at the Oswego County Airport, Route 176 North.
Modified GE MASTR II with NHRC Controller. ~25 W.
Owned by Oswego County Emergency Management Office (OCEC/RACES).
K2QQY
442.350 MHz		 Receive: 442.350 MHz   Transmit: 447.350 MHz   PL Tone: 103.5
Located at the Scriba Site, ~½ mile East of CR 29/51A intersection. ~660 ft ASL.
GE MASTR II. NHRC Controller. ~50 W.
Part of the New York Statewide Linked Repeater System.

Nets

  • Pathfinder Net — Weeknights at 9:00 PM on 147.39 MHz (WN8Z repeater, temporarily while 147.15 is repaired). Amateur fellowship net — all calls welcome.
  • Oswego County Emergency Communicators — Normally 3rd Sunday of each month (except December) on the 147.15 MHz repeater. Schedule may vary to avoid conflicts.
  • SSTV Net — Every Saturday evening at 7:30 PM on the 147.39 MHz repeater.
Note: The 147.150 repeater was temporarily moved. The Pathfinder Net is currently on 147.39 (WN8Z) while repairs are completed. Contact the RACES Radio Officer for current status.

Coverage & Usage

For information about the repeaters or to report issues, contact the OCEC/RACES Radio Officer: Michael KD2DVC.

During RACES activations, all stations are asked to exercise patience and good operating practices, giving emergency traffic priority.
